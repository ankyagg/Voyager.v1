Jaipur Travel Knowledge

Overview
--------
Jaipur, the "Pink City", is the capital of Rajasthan and a UNESCO World Heritage City (2019).
Part of the Golden Triangle tourist circuit (Delhi-Agra-Jaipur).
Famous for its stunning Rajput architecture, vibrant bazaars, and rich culture.
The city gets its name from the pink sandstone used in its buildings since 1876.

Best Time to Visit
------------------
October to March: Cool, pleasant weather (8-25°C), ideal for sightseeing.
April-June: Very hot (35-45°C), avoid midday outings.
Monsoon (July-September): Moderate rain, lush greenery, fewer tourists.
Best months: November, December, January, February.

Top Attractions
---------------
- Amber Fort (Amer Fort): Magnificent hilltop fort-palace with mirror work, built 1592.
  Entry: ₹200 (Indians), ₹500 (foreigners). Elephant ride: ₹900 (restricted, ethical concerns exist).
- City Palace: Royal palace complex with museums, still partially home to Jaipur's royal family.
- Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds): Iconic 5-story pink sandstone facade with 953 windows.
- Jantar Mantar: UNESCO World Heritage astronomical observatory built by Maharaja Jai Singh II.
- Nahargarh Fort: Offers panoramic views of Jaipur; great sunset spot.
- Jaigarh Fort: Connected to Amber Fort; houses the world's largest wheeled cannon.
- Jal Mahal: Palace appearing to float in the middle of Man Sagar Lake.
- Albert Hall Museum: Oldest museum in Rajasthan with impressive artifacts.
- Chokhi Dhani: Ethnic village resort with Rajasthani folk performances and cuisine.
- Birla Mandir: Modern white marble temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu.

Shopping
--------
Johari Bazaar: Jewelry (kundan, meenakari, diamonds) — don't miss the gem merchants.
Bapu Bazaar: Textiles, jootis (leather shoes), handicrafts, blue pottery.
Tripolia Bazaar: Lac bangles, textiles, and metalwork.
Nehru Bazaar: Textiles and clothing. Always bargain — start at 40% of asking price.

Local Cuisine
-------------
Must-try: Dal Baati Churma (iconic Rajasthani dish), Laal Maas (spicy mutton), Gatte ki Sabzi.
Sweets: Ghewar, Malpua, Rasgulla from local sweet shops.
Street food: Pyaaz Kachori at Rawat Mishthan Bhandar is legendary.
Thali meals: Traditional unlimited Rajasthani thali from ₹200-400.

Transport
---------
By air: Jaipur International Airport, 12km from city centre.
By rail: Jaipur Junction, well connected to Delhi (4.5h), Mumbai (18h), Agra (4h).
Local: Auto-rickshaws, cycle rickshaws, app-based cabs (Uber/Ola available).
Day trips: Amber Fort by local bus (₹15) or taxi (₹300-400 one way).

Accommodation
-------------
Budget: Hostels and guesthouses in old city from ₹500-1500/night.
Heritage hotels: Converted havelis and forts from ₹3000-10000/night.
Luxury: Rambagh Palace, Samode Palace ₹15000-50000/night.

Budget Tips (₹ per person per day)
------------------------------------
Entry fees (Amber + City Palace + Hawa Mahal + Jantar): ~₹1200 combined.
Local meals: ₹200-500 per day.
Auto-rickshaw city tour (half day): ₹400-600.
Total daily budget: ₹1500-2500 budget / ₹3000-6000 mid-range.

Travel Tips
-----------
1. Visit Amber Fort at opening time (8am) to beat crowds and harsh afternoon heat.
2. Purchase the Jaipur City Palace composite ticket for better value.
3. Hire a licensed government guide for historical sites (₹300-500 for 2-3 hours).
4. Learn a few bargaining phrases — shopkeepers appreciate the effort.
5. Respect dress codes at temples and palaces (cover shoulders and knees).
6. Watch out for touts at popular sites; only deal with authorized guides.
7. The Heritage Walk (organized by RTDC) is an excellent introduction to the old city.
