Kerala Travel Knowledge

Overview
--------
Kerala, "God's Own Country", is a lush tropical state on India's southwestern coast.
Famous for tranquil backwaters, emerald hill stations, Ayurvedic spa treatments, and wildlife.
Part of the Western Ghats biodiversity hotspot; rich in spices, tea, and rubber plantations.
Kerala has the highest literacy rate in India and is known for its culture and cuisine.

Best Time to Visit
------------------
Peak season (September-March): Post-monsoon, lush and green, ideal weather (22-33°C).
Best months: October, November, December (festivals, backwaters at their best).
Summer (April-May): Hot and humid but manageable; Thrissur Pooram festival in April-May.
Monsoon (June-August): Heavy rains; Ayurvedic treatments are most effective; beautiful greenery.

Top Attractions
---------------
- Alleppey (Alappuzha) Backwaters: Houseboat cruises on a network of serene lagoons and canals.
  Houseboat rates: ₹8000-20000 per night (1-3 bedroom, all meals included).
- Munnar: High-altitude hill station at 1,600m in the Western Ghats with vast tea estates.
  Famous for: Eravikulam National Park (Nilgiri Tahr), Mattupetty Dam, Top Station.
- Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary (Thekkady): Tiger reserve with boat safaris on Periyar Lake.
  Entry: ₹200, boat safari: ₹150; bamboo rafting available.
- Varkala Beach: Unique clifftop beach, mix of pilgrimage site and tourist hub.
- Fort Kochi: Colonial quarter with Chinese fishing nets, Dutch Palace, Jewish Synagogue.
- Athirapally Waterfalls: "Bhavana Vana" — India's own Niagara, used in many films.
- Kovalam Beach: Famous lighthouse beach, excellent for swimming and Ayurvedic treatments.
- Bekal Fort: Largest fort in Kerala, with stunning views of the Arabian Sea.
- Kumarakom Bird Sanctuary: Paradise for migratory birds; excellent birding November-February.
- Wayanad: Tribal heritage, Edakkal Caves, Chembra Peak trek, wildlife safaris.

Backwaters
----------
Houseboat network: 900km of waterways connecting Alleppey, Kumarakom, Kollam.
Day cruise routes: Alleppey to Kumarakom (8 hours, ₹5000-8000 per boat).
Overnight houseboat: Most popular experience; book directly at ferry terminal or advance.
Village canoe rides: ₹300-800 for a manual canoe through narrow village canals.

Ayurveda
--------
Kerala is the authentic home of Ayurvedic treatment.
Panchakarma (detox treatment): 7-21 days at certified Ayurveda centres.
Day treatments: Shirodhara, Abhyanga massage available at beach resorts.
Authentic centres: Somatheeram, Kairali Ayurvedic Health Village.

Local Cuisine
-------------
Signature dishes: Kerala fish curry (with kudampuli tamarind), prawn moilee, appam with stew.
Sadhya: Grand traditional feast on banana leaf with 24+ dishes, served during Onam.
Kerala Parotta with beef or chicken curry is popular street food.
Toddy (palm wine) and coconut-based dishes are local specialties.
Filter coffee and fresh coconut water are must-tries.

Transport
---------
By air: Kochi International Airport (busiest), Trivandrum, Calicut airports.
By rail: Well-connected coastal railway; scenic Nilambur-Shoranur line through forests.
By water: KSRTC water ferries for backwater travel; very affordable (₹10-50).
Local: Auto-rickshaws, Uber/Ola in cities. Renting a car recommended for Western Ghats.

Budget Tips (₹ per person per day)
------------------------------------
Budget homestay: ₹800-2000/night, usually includes breakfast and dinner.
Local meals: ₹150-400 per day at small restaurants.
Boat rides: ₹150-500 per person.
Total daily budget: ₹1500-3000 budget / ₹3000-7000 mid-range.

Travel Tips
-----------
1. Book houseboats directly at Alleppey boat jetty to avoid heavy middleman commissions.
2. Visit Periyar boat safari in the early morning (6am) for best wildlife sightings.
3. Pack light cotton clothes and strong mosquito repellent for backwater regions.
4. Many areas have no ATMs; carry sufficient cash especially in Wayanad and Munnar.
5. Onam (August-September) is the best time for cultural immersion.
6. Authentic Kathakali performances are available in Kochi (Fort area) every evening.
7. Always ask for government-certified guides at wildlife sanctuaries.
