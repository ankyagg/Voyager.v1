Ladakh Travel Knowledge

Overview
--------
Ladakh is a high-altitude union territory in northern India, bordered by Tibet and Pakistan.
Known as the "Land of High Passes", it sits at an average altitude of 3,500m above sea level.
One of the world's most spectacular landscapes — barren mountains, turquoise lakes, ancient monasteries.
Attracts adventure seekers, motorcyclists, trekkers, and spiritual travellers from across the globe.

Best Time to Visit
------------------
June to September: Only time most roads and passes are open; ideal for trekking and biking.
July-August: Peak season; Pangong Lake accessible, Zanskar Valley open.
Winter (November-February): Extremely cold (-20 to -30°C at night); Chadar Trek on frozen Zanskar River.
Avoid: May (Khardung La and Rohtang may be closed); September-end for road closures risk.

Top Attractions
---------------
- Pangong Tso Lake: Stunning 134km-long lake straddling India-China border (3,4000m altitude).
  Famous from the film 3 Idiots; water changes colour from blue to green to red throughout the day.
- Nubra Valley: Sand dunes with Bactrian camels at Hunder; Diskit Monastery (32m Maitreya Buddha).
- Khardung La Pass (5,359m): One of the world's highest motorable roads; mandatory stop.
- Magnetic Hill: Optical illusion where vehicles appear to defy gravity (20km from Leh).
- Hemis Monastery: Largest and wealthiest monastery; Hemis Festival (June/July) is spectacular.
- Thiksey Monastery: 12-story monastery resembling Potala Palace of Tibet.
- Alchi Monastery: 1000-year-old monastery with the finest early Kashmiri Buddhist art.
- Zanskar Valley: Remote river valley, accessible by jeep or the famous Chadar winter trek.
- Shanti Stupa: White-domed Buddhist stupa offering panoramic views of Leh.
- Leh Palace: 17-story ruined palace modelled after Potala Palace, Leh city.

Adventure Activities
--------------------
Trekking: Markha Valley Trek (4-7 days), Stok Kangri (6,122m summit), Chadar Trek (frozen river, Jan-Feb).
Motorcycling: Manali-Leh Highway (475km over 2-3 days), Leh-Srinagar-Leh circuit.
River rafting: Indus and Zanskar rivers; Grade III-IV rapids near Phey village.
Mountain biking: Challenging high-altitude routes.
Camel safaris: Nubra Valley sand dunes.

Local Cuisine
-------------
Must-try: Thukpa (Tibetan noodle soup), Momos (steamed dumplings), Skyu (pasta stew).
Butter tea (Po Cha) — salty, acquired taste; essential for altitude acclimatisation.
Tsampa (roasted barley flour), dried apricots (Ladakh's famous export).
Chang (local barley beer) at local homes and festivals.

Transport
---------
By air: Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport, Leh; flights from Delhi (1h), Mumbai (2.5h).
By road: Manali-Leh Highway (475km, open June-October); Srinagar-Leh (434km).
Local: Taxis from Leh taxi stand (fixed government rates); jeep safaris for remote areas.
Inner Line Permits: Required for Pangong, Nubra, and Hanle; arrange in Leh (₹400 + process fees).

Acclimatisation (Critical)
--------------------------
Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) is serious — follow these rules:
Day 1 in Leh: Rest completely. Do not exert yourself.
Day 2: Short walks only. Drink 3-4 litres water daily.
Day 3 onwards: Gentle acclimatisation drives to moderate altitude.
Carry Diamox (acetazolamide) tablets; consult a doctor before the trip.
Travel directly from sea level to Leh only by flight; do NOT proceed higher same day.

Accommodation
-------------
Budget: Guesthouses in Leh from ₹600-2000/night.
Mid-range: Boutique camps and eco-lodges ₹3000-8000/night.
Luxury glamping: Pangong Lake camps ₹8000-20000/night (all meals, lake view).

Budget Tips (₹ per person per day)
------------------------------------
Basic meals at local restaurants: ₹400-700.
Shared jeep Leh-Pangong round trip (2 days): ₹2500-3500/person.
Inner Line Permit: ₹400 one-time.
Total daily budget: ₹2000-4000 budget / ₹5000-12000 mid-range.

Travel Tips
-----------
1. ACCLIMATISE — do not underestimate AMS; it can be life-threatening.
2. Carry a first aid kit with Diamox, ORS, pain killers, and bandages.
3. Sun protection is essential: UV radiation is extreme at high altitude.
4. Fuel up in Leh — petrol pumps are scarce on remote routes.
5. Download offline maps (OSMAnd or Maps.me) — mobile signal is patchy.
6. Book permits for restricted areas 2-3 days in advance in Leh city.
7. Carry warm layers even in summer — temperatures drop below 5°C at night.
8. Carry enough cash; ATMs are limited and often out of service.
