Manali Travel Knowledge

Overview
--------
Manali is a high-altitude Himalayan resort town in Himachal Pradesh, India, situated at 2,050m.
Known as the "Valley of Gods", it is a gateway to Lahaul-Spiti, Leh-Ladakh, and Rohtang Pass.
Adventure capital of North India — attracts trekkers, skiers, bikers, and honeymooners year-round.

Best Time to Visit
------------------
Summer (March-June): Best for sightseeing, trekking, and river rafting. Temperature: 10-25°C.
Monsoon (July-September): Landslide risk on mountain roads; avoid Rohtang Pass.
Winter (October-February): Heavy snow, Rohtang Pass closes (Nov-May), but skiing is excellent.
Snow season (Jan-Feb): Solang Valley turns into a skiing paradise.

Top Attractions
---------------
- Solang Valley: 14km from Manali, famous for skiing, paragliding, zorbing, and snow activities.
- Rohtang Pass (3,978m): Scenic high-altitude pass with panoramic Himalayan views; permits required.
- Hadimba Devi Temple: 500-year-old cedar wood temple surrounded by ancient deodar forest.
- Mall Road: The main bazaar for shopping, local food stalls, cafes, and woolens.
- Beas River Rafting: White water rafting on Grade III-IV rapids near Pirdi.
- Naggar Castle: Medieval fort-castle 20km from Manali with mountain views.
- Vashisht Hot Springs: Ancient sulfurous hot springs believed to have healing properties.
- Great Himalayan National Park: UNESCO site, excellent for treks and wildlife spotting.
- Hampta Pass Trek: 4-day moderate trek through dramatic Himalayan landscapes.
- Kullu Valley: 40km south, famous for the Kullu Dussehra festival and apple orchards.

Adventure Activities
--------------------
Trekking routes: Beas Kund, Hampta Pass, Chandrakhani Pass, Pin Parvati Pass.
Skiing: Solang Valley (beginner/intermediate), Rohtang Pass (advanced).
Paragliding: Solang Valley; ₹1500-2500 per flight.
River rafting: Beas River near Pirdi (Grade III-IV); ₹600-1000 pp.
Zorbing and ATVs: Available at Solang Valley; ₹500-1000.

Local Cuisine
-------------
Must-try: Dham (traditional Himachali feast), Sidu (local bread with ghee), Trout fish curry.
Cafes on Mall Road serve excellent Maggi noodles, thukpa (Tibetan noodle soup), momos.
Try local Himachali apple juice and apple cider.

Transport
---------
By road: 570km from Delhi via NH-3 (Manali-Leh Highway), approx 12-14 hours.
By air: Nearest airport is Bhuntar (Kullu-Manali Airport), 50km away.
Local transport: Taxis and shared jeeps available; roads can be icy in winter.
Rohtang Pass: Personal vehicles require permits, only available on alternate days.

Accommodation
-------------
Budget: Guesthouses in Old Manali from ₹500-1500/night.
Mid-range: Hotels on Mall Road ₹2000-5000/night.
Luxury: Span Resort, Solang Valley resorts ₹8000-20000/night.
Book accommodation 2-3 months ahead for peak summer (May-June).

Budget Tips (₹ per person per day)
------------------------------------
Local dhabas / Tibetan restaurants: ₹300-600.
Transport to Rohtang: ₹2000-3000/group (shared).
Adventure activities: ₹1000-3000 depending on activity.
Total daily budget: ₹1500-3000 budget / ₹3000-8000 mid-range.

Travel Tips
-----------
1. Carry warm clothing even in summer — mountain weather changes fast.
2. Acclimatise for half a day before attempting high-altitude excursions.
3. Book Rohtang Pass permit online at rohtangpermit.nic.in; very limited daily quota.
4. Carry cash — ATMs can be unreliable in remote areas.
5. Old Manali (across the river) is more relaxed and has better cafes.
6. Hire a reliable local guide for high-altitude treks.
7. Carry altitude sickness medication (Diamox) if planning to go above 4000m.
